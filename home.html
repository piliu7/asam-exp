A lot of times, people are surprised that my family has been in the states for so long. A lot of my white friends are surprised that I’m more American than them.

Katherine, 20




There was a pride in me. ‘Nice! I don’t have to be part of [ESL] anymore. I can actually go and do the real thing with other students.’ Not pride...it felt like freedom from being conformed…

Peter, 21

Photo by Priscilla Liu




Photo by Priscilla Liu

Growing up in Iowa, I always had this … inarticulatable sense of being slightly out of place, and not really understanding why.

Cheryl, 21




The people from Hong Kong recognize me as a type of person that exists in Hong Kong. The problem is people not from Hong Kong who view me as Americanized in a way that people in Hong Kong wouldn’t agree with and I don’t see at all.

Corey, 21

Photo by Danielle Oberdier




Photo by Danielle Oberdier

In China, I have more influence among my friends... I felt in high school I was more of a participant in campus culture. Here, I feel like an outsider who is treated nicely but isn’t necessarily involved.

Joyce, 22




I grew up understanding that I couldn’t have a voice, whether it was at home amongst Asian Americans or whether it was amongst white people.

Yuchi, 21
